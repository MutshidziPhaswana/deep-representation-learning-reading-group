# 🧠 Deep Representation Learning Reading Group

Welcome to the **Deep Representation Learning reading group!**
We explore how deep models discover meaningful representations from complex, unlabeled, or shifting data.

---

## 🌱 Motivation & Community

Research in the lab is often a 1-on-1 journey between a student and the supervisor, which can sometimes feel isolating.

We are starting this reading group to bridge that gap and build a community.

Our goal is to create a space where students feel energized, share ideas across different projects, and gain fresh insights through peer discussions. It's about breaking out of our silos and learning from each other.

---

## 🛋️ Our Low-Friction Culture

We know everyone is busy. To make sure this group is a source of energy rather than a source of stress, we run on three strict rules:

* **Zero Prep for Attendees**
  You do not need to read the paper beforehand. Just show up with a coffee, listen, and jump into the discussion.

* **No Slides Required**
  Presenters do not need to waste hours building beautiful slide decks. Walking through a highlighted PDF is completely acceptable and highly encouraged.

* **Strict Timeboxing**
  Presentations are ruthlessly capped at 20 minutes. We are here for the community discussion, not a lecture.

## ⚙️ How We Run Sessions

The presenter’s job is **not** to summarize the paper line-by-line. Instead, give the group the TL;DR by focusing on:

* **The Core Intuition**
  What is the fundamental problem and the elegant idea behind the solution?

* **The Mechanism**
  Walk through the most important equation, loss function, or architectural choice.

* **The Lab Connection**
  How does this apply to our work?
  *(e.g., Can this contrastive loss be used for our open-world detection? Does this continual learning technique solve our model fusion interference?)*

## 🗺️ Thematic Focus Areas

We will rotate our sessions through the lab's active research areas in Deep Representation Learning https://tvanzyl.github.io/research/:

* 🌶️ **Self-supervised Learning**
  Discovering meaningful representations from unlabeled/weakly labeled data.

* 🔥 **Time Series Modelling**
  Deep learning in forecasting beyond historical benchmarks.

* 🌶️ **Incremental & Continual Learning**
  Addressing catastrophic forgetting in lifelong learning.

* **Open World Detection**
  Anomalies, out-of-domain data, and one-class learning.

* **Model Fusion**
  Modular DL, knowledge distillation, domain adaptation, and federated learning.

* **Contrastive / Metric / Similarity Learning & Learning to Rank**
  Enhancing accuracy in retrieval and recommendation.

* **Surrogate Assisted & Amortized Optimization**
  Combining ML with agent-based simulation and evolutionary optimization.

* **One-, Few-shot Learning & Test-Time Adaptation**
  Generalizing to new tasks with ≤ 5 samples per class.
